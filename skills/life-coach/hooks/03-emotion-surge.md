# Hook 3 — Emotion Surge 🟠

**Priority**: HIGH — fire at the next natural pause in the conversation
**Status**: Active in all modes. In Maintenance Mode, treat with the same care — maintenance sessions can surface unexpected depth.

---

## Trigger Conditions

Fire when there is a sudden shift in emotional register — unexpected and disproportionate to the apparent prompt.

**Explicit signals**:
- Client mentions tears, crying, or emotion unexpectedly: "sorry, I don't know why I'm emotional"
- Sudden anger in a previously calm exchange
- *"That hit differently"* or *"I didn't expect that"*
- *"Can we stop for a moment"*
- *"I need a second"*
- Disclosure of something they clearly haven't told many people

**Implicit signals**:
- Very long pause before responding to a question that seemed routine
- Response significantly shorter and flatter than preceding messages
- *"I'm fine"* or *"it's nothing"* after an emotionally loaded exchange
- An answer that trails off or is self-interrupted
- A sudden change to formal, distancing language ("one might feel..." instead of "I feel...")

---

## Detection Nuances

**The question that triggered the surge is usually important**:
Don't abandon the question. File it. After the emotion settles, you will often return to exactly that question — because it found something real.

**Distinguish emotion surge from momentary discomfort**:
- Discomfort: brief, client recovers and continues with similar energy
- Surge: sustained shift in tone, pace, or engagement that doesn't self-resolve in 1–2 exchanges

**Text/chat contexts**:
Watch for: sudden brevity, ellipses, very long delays between messages, "nvm" or "it's fine" after raising something.

---

## Response Protocol

**Step 1 — Stop the current line of inquiry immediately**
Do not finish the thought you were building. The emotion IS the session now.

**Step 2 — Stay with it**:
*"Something just moved in you. We don't need to analyze it — just stay with it for a moment.
What's there?"*

Do NOT:
- Rush to label the emotion for them
- Offer the insight immediately
- Ask "why" in the first moment — "why" triggers the analytical mind and the client leaves the feeling

**Step 3 — Give it space**:
Silence is appropriate here. If the client doesn't respond quickly, that is not a problem.
After genuine space: *"Take your time."*

**Step 4 — Invite articulation only after the surge settles**:
*"What did that bring up that we haven't named yet?"*

**Step 5 — Connect to the session**:
Once articulated, connect the emotion to the actual work:
*"This is important. This feeling — [what they named] — is usually sitting right at the edge of what needs to change. What does it say about what matters most here?"*

---

## What NOT To Do

- Do not comfort the emotion away: "it's okay, don't worry about it"
- Do not rush to the insight: the emotion is not a problem to be solved, it's information to be received
- Do not ask multiple questions in quick succession — one question, then wait
- Do not apologize for causing the emotion: "sorry for bringing that up" implies you shouldn't have

---

## Return to Session

After the surge has been received and articulated, assess readiness to continue:
*"Are you okay to keep going — or do you need a moment before we move on?"*
Honor whatever they say. If they need to stop: close the session using `/close`.
If they continue: the session direction has likely shifted. Follow their lead, not your agenda.
