# Hook 2 — Resistance Detector 🟠

**Priority**: HIGH — fire at the next natural pause in the conversation
**Status**: Active in Discovery Mode and Deep Work Mode. Active in Maintenance Mode only if new challenge emerges.

---

## Trigger Conditions

Fire when the client pushes back on a diagnosis, reframing, or exercise **2 or more times consecutively**,
OR when answers become progressively shorter and more deflective.

**Explicit resistance signals**:
- "yes but..." followed by the same concern
- "I know, but..." (signals they've heard but aren't integrating)
- "I've tried that" (without specifics of what happened)
- "that's not really it" (without offering what IS it)
- "I hear you, but..."
- "It's complicated" repeated 2+ times

**Implicit resistance signals**:
- Monosyllabic responses after previously engaged, detailed answers
- Subject changes precisely when a topic gets emotionally close
- Increased use of hypotheticals: "if someone were in my situation..." (distancing)
- Sudden intellectualizing after emotional content surfaced
- Agreeing too quickly: "yes, exactly" right after something challenging — may be deflection

---

## Detection Nuances

**The 2-strike rule**: One pushback is feedback. Two consecutive pushbacks are a pattern.
Do not fire this hook on the first pushback — that may be legitimate correction.

**Resistance vs. Correction**:
- Correction: client says what you got wrong AND offers an alternative. Honor it.
- Resistance: client says what's wrong but offers nothing. This is the hook.

**Passive resistance is still resistance**:
Short answers, topic changes, and sudden intellectualizing are forms of avoidance.
The hook should fire on these even when explicit pushback language isn't present.

---

## Response Protocol

**Do NOT push harder.** Resistance is information, not obstruction.

**Step 1 — Name the pattern without judgment**:
*"I notice I keep hitting something when we approach [topic]. I'm curious about that —
not frustrated, just curious. What feels wrong about the direction we've been going?"*

**Step 2 — Let them redirect**:
The client's explanation of the resistance usually contains the real issue.
Listen for: what they say AND what they avoid saying.

**Step 3 — Re-run Phase 3**:
Load `references/diagnostic-deep.md`.
The block has likely shifted or there's a deeper layer below the original diagnosis.
Do not proceed with the old diagnosis — update it.

**Step 4 — Test the new direction**:
Once they've named what they're resisting, check: *"What would it mean if [what they offered] were actually the issue? Does that feel more true?"*

---

## What NOT To Do

- Do not repeat the same reframe louder or with more conviction
- Do not interpret resistance as failure — it is the most important diagnostic signal
- Do not skip this hook because the session is going well otherwise
- Do not pathologize resistance: "you're being defensive" is not useful coaching language

---

## Return to Session

After resolving the resistance signal:
*"Let's see if we can find the edge that actually matters to you. Where do you want to go from here?"*
Let the client set the direction. Their choice reveals their readiness.
