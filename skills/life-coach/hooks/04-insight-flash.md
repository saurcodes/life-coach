# Hook 4 — Insight Flash ✨

**Priority**: HIGH — fire at the next natural pause; do not interrupt the flash itself
**Status**: Active in all modes.

---

## Trigger Conditions

Fire when the client has a sudden self-recognition — something they say indicates they've seen something new about themselves, often mid-sentence or mid-answer.

**Explicit signals**:
- "oh..." (often followed by silence or a trailing thought)
- "wait, I never thought of it that way"
- "that's actually..."
- "huh" (genuine, not dismissive)
- Client repeats the insight back to themselves, slightly changed, as if testing it
- "I just realized..."
- "I've never said that out loud before"

**Implicit signals**:
- A statement that clearly surprises even the person saying it — they pause, re-read, trail off
- An answer that contradicts something they said earlier, delivered without defensiveness — their thinking has shifted
- Laughter at something that isn't a joke — the laughter of recognition
- A sudden shift from past-tense to present-tense language: "I was afraid... I *am* afraid"

---

## Detection Nuances

**An insight flash is NOT**:
- A moment of agreement with something you said ("yes, exactly") — that may be deflection
- An intellectual observation about their situation — insights feel, not just think
- A restatement of something discussed previously — it's new even if the words are familiar

**The flash is often incomplete**:
The client will surface the insight before they can fully articulate it.
Your role is to slow it down, not finish it for them.

---

## Response Protocol

**Step 1 — Do not fill the silence**
The impulse to affirm, reflect back, or build on the insight immediately is the wrong move.
Wait. The insight needs to land before it can be used.

**Step 2 — Do not rush to affirm**
"That's exactly right!" immediately after an insight closes the space and makes it yours.
Let it be theirs.

**Step 3 — Invite them to stay with it**:
*"Stay with that. What just happened?"*

**Step 4 — After they articulate it, slow it further**:
*"Say that again — but slower."*
This instruction anchors the insight. Saying something slowly forces embodiment.

**Step 5 — This is a Phase 7b micro-test moment**
The insight needs to be anchored before moving on.
After they've stated it slowly: *"What changes if that's true?"*
This moves the insight from intellectual observation to implication.

**Step 6 — Connect to action**:
*"If you held onto that — just that — what would you do differently tomorrow?"*

---

## What NOT To Do

- Do not interpret the insight for them — let them say what they saw
- Do not immediately pivot to the next question — the insight IS the content
- Do not add your perspective on the insight immediately after it lands — you'll dilute it
- Do not move to closing the session on the high of an insight without checking what it actually asks of the client

---

## Return to Session

After the insight is anchored, check whether it changes the session's direction:
*"Does what you just named change what you want to focus on for the rest of our time today?"*
An insight often reveals that the presenting problem was a proxy — the real work may now be different.
