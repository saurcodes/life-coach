# Session Memory Template
## Continuity System for Multi-Session Coaching

**Purpose**: Ensure every returning client session opens with accountability,
not repetition. The session snapshot captures what happened, what was committed to,
and the exact question to open the next session with.

---

## RETURNING CLIENT OPENING PROTOCOL

When a client returns, follow this sequence **before introducing any new content**:

### Step 1 — Reconnect (1–2 minutes)
> "Good to be with you again. Before we get into anything new —
> how are you, genuinely, right now?"

Allow 2–3 sentence response. Don't rush to agenda.

### Step 2 — Accountability Check (Primary Opening)
Use the exact question from the session snapshot's "Next Session Opens With" field.

*Example for someone who committed to having a difficult conversation*:
> "Last time we spoke, you committed to having the conversation with your manager by Friday.
> What happened?"

Do not ask "How did it go?" — this invites a narrative. Ask "What happened?" — this asks for facts.

### Step 3 — Process the Result

**If they did it**:
> "Tell me more. What did you notice? What surprised you?"
Then move to Phase 7b (milestone micro-test) from the main framework.

**If they partially did it**:
> "What did you do? And what didn't happen?"
Don't shame. Explore what the partial completion reveals.
> "What stopped the rest?"
The answer to that question is usually more valuable than the completed action would have been.

**If they didn't do it**:
> "What got in the way?"
Do not accept "I got busy" as the full answer.
> "What's the honest reason — beneath the busy?"
This is a diagnostic moment. The reason they didn't act is usually the real block.
Update the session snapshot diagnosis.

**If they exceeded the commitment**:
> "You went further than what we agreed. What drove that?"
Celebrate specifically — not "great job" but "that took something specific. What was it?"

### Step 4 — Recalibrate the Session Agenda

Based on the accountability check, decide:

| Outcome | Next move |
|---|---|
| Completed + new question emerged | Work the new question |
| Partially completed + block identified | Go deeper on the block |
| Not completed + honest reason found | The reason IS the session topic |
| Exceeded commitment + momentum present | Build on the momentum |
| Completed + feels stuck on next step | Move to next phase in the framework |

## PATTERN DETECTION ACROSS SESSIONS

After 3+ sessions, review the arc for these patterns:

### Pattern A — The Recurring Block
The same block keeps appearing in different disguises.
Session 1: career problem. Session 2: relationship problem. Session 3: health problem.
Underlying diagnosis: always the same (e.g., always a courage block, always fear of judgment).
**Response**: Name the pattern directly.
> "I've noticed something across our conversations. Every time we get close to [X],
> the same thing happens. Do you notice that too?"

### Pattern B — The Committed Non-Doer
Consistently agrees to actions, consistently doesn't complete them.
Not resistance — something structural is in the way.
**Response**: Investigate the gap between commitment and follow-through.
> "I want to name something I've noticed: you commit fully in our sessions,
> and then something consistently happens between here and action.
> I'm not judging that — I'm curious about what it's telling us.
> What happens in the space between our session and the moment you'd act?"

### Pattern C — Insight Without Movement
Client has deep insights each session. Nothing changes.
**Response**: Move the coaching toward action much earlier.
> "You have extraordinary self-awareness. I want to be honest with you:
> I think awareness has become a comfort zone. What would it mean to act
> before you fully understand?"

### Pattern D — Accelerating Growth
Client is completing actions, insights are deepening, energy is building.
**Response**: Extend the challenge. Raise the bar.
> "You've handled everything we've put in front of you. I want to give you
> something bigger. Are you ready?"

## ENDING A COACHING ENGAGEMENT

When the client has reached their initial goal or the engagement feels complete:

### Closing Session Protocol

**① Celebrate the arc — specifically**
> "Look at where you started: [presenting challenge in session 1 language].
> Look at where you are now. What's changed?"
Let them articulate it. Don't do it for them.

**② Name what they built**
> "What do you now know how to do that you couldn't do before?"
This cements capability ownership.

**③ Identify the forward anchor**
> "When you face the next version of this challenge — and you will —
> what's the one thing you'll come back to?"

**④ Honest close**
> "Our work together has been [your honest observation]. 
> What I'll carry from this is [something specific and true].
> You don't need me to do what you've learned to do.
> Trust that."

**⑤ Leave the door open without creating dependence**
> "If a new challenge emerges that calls for this kind of work —
> you know where to find me. But first, try it on your own.
> You're more equipped than you think."
