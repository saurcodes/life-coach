# Exercise Library
## 25 Coaching Exercises with Full Scripts

**Usage rule**: Select ONE exercise per session. Match to the diagnosed block type.
Give precise instructions — vague exercises don't get done.
Always frame the exercise as data collection, not performance.

## CLARITY EXERCISES

### C1 — The Deathbed Reflection
**Best for**: Chronic indecision, fear of choosing wrong, paralysis between options
**Time required**: 20–30 minutes, single sitting
**Archetype fit**: All archetypes; particularly powerful for Achievers and Philosophers

**Full script to give the client**:
> "Find a quiet place where you won't be interrupted. Close your eyes and take 3 slow breaths.
> Now imagine yourself at 85 years old — healthy, clear-minded, looking back at your life.
> From that vantage point, you're reviewing the decision you're facing today.
> Hold both paths in your mind — the one you're considering and the one you'd take if you chose differently.
> Ask yourself: which choice, looking back, would I regret more?
> Don't analyze. Just notice what arises.
> Write exactly one page on what that older version of yourself would say to you today.
> Don't edit. Don't make it sound wise. Just write what comes."

**What to bring back**: The one sentence that surprised you most.
**Debrief question**: "What did the 85-year-old know that the current you is avoiding?"

### C2 — Neti Neti (Not This, Not This)
**Best for**: Too many options, can't identify what they actually want, overwhelmed by possibility
**Time required**: 15–20 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Analyst, Integrator, Philosopher

**Full script**:
> "Take a blank page and draw a line down the middle.
> On the left: write every option you're considering — every direction you've thought about.
> On the right: for each one, write your honest gut response: does it energize or deplete you?
> Then, begin crossing things out — not what's unrealistic, but what you know in your gut is not right for you.
> Keep crossing until you can't cross any more without lying to yourself.
> What remains is your signal. It may not be certain — but it's directional."

**What to bring back**: What's left after all the crossing out. And the feeling that came with each elimination.
**Debrief question**: "Which item was hardest to cross out — and what does that tell you?"

### C3 — The Energy Audit
**Best for**: Career crossroads, "is this still right for me?", chronic low motivation
**Time required**: 7 days of tracking, 20 minutes review (3-day version available)
**Archetype fit**: Thinker (loves the data), Doer, Strategist

**Full script**:
> "For the next 7 days, carry a small notebook or use your phone.
> Three times a day — morning, midday, evening — write:
> 1. What did you just spend time on?
> 2. Energy level after: +1 (energized), 0 (neutral), -1 (depleted)
> No analysis. Just record.
> At the end of day 7, tally: which activities consistently give you +1?
> Which consistently give you -1?
> The data will tell you something your mind has been rationalizing around."

**3-day version** (if 7 days isn't feasible): Same exercise, 3 days only. Less pattern confidence
but still directional. Tell the client: "3 days gives us a signal. 7 days gives us a pattern.
Start with 3 — and if it's inconclusive, extend."

**What to bring back**: The 3 highest-energy activities and the 3 most depleting. Pattern noticed.
**Debrief question**: "How much of your current life is built around the -1 column?"

### C4 — The Two-Column Letter
**Best for**: Values conflict, can't decide between staying and leaving (job, relationship, city)
**Time required**: 45 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Caregiver, Thinker, Seeker

**Full script**:
> "You're going to write two letters — one page each.
> Letter One: Write to yourself from the version of you who chose Option A.
> It's 2 years later. You chose it. Tell yourself: what is life like? What are you glad about?
> What was hard? What do you miss from Option B?
> Letter Two: Write to yourself from the version of you who chose Option B.
> Same questions. 2 years later. What is life like? What are you glad about? What was hard?
> Read both letters back as if a friend wrote them. Don't decide — just listen."

**What to bring back**: Which letter felt more real when you read it back.
**Debrief question**: "Which version of yourself felt more like *you*?"

### C5 — The Five Whys (Personal Edition)
**Best for**: Surface-level presenting problem that keeps recurring — something beneath hasn't been named
**Time required**: 15 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Thinker, Strategist

**Full script**:
> "State the problem in one sentence. Write it down.
> Then ask yourself: why does this problem exist? Write one answer.
> Then ask: why does THAT exist? Write one answer.
> Do this five times — each 'why' going one level deeper than the last.
> Most people find that by the 4th or 5th why, they've arrived at something
> they haven't said out loud before.
> Don't skip levels. Don't summarize. Go one step at a time."

**What to bring back**: The 4th and 5th answer.
**Debrief question**: "When you arrived at that 5th why — did it surprise you?"

### C6 — The Chanakya Council
**Best for**: Complex decision where one perspective keeps dominating; stuck in one viewpoint despite good analysis
**Time required**: 30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Strategist, Thinker, Doer (who needs to slow down and hear the other voices)

**Full script**:
> "Imagine a council of three advisors sitting across from you.
> The first: A wise elder who has seen everything and is no longer afraid of anything.
> The second: A bold entrepreneur who has failed magnificently and rebuilt.
> The third: A cautious pragmatist who protects what matters most.
> Each one has read your situation completely.
> Write what each of them says to you — not what you think they'd say,
> what they actually say if they were speaking without filter.
> Now: which voice do you most need to listen to? Which have you been ignoring?"

**What to bring back**: The voice you've been ignoring and why.
**Debrief question**: "What would you do if you followed that voice — just for the next step?"

## CAPABILITY EXERCISES

### K1 — The Capability Audit
**Best for**: Imposter syndrome, undervaluing proven track record, "I'm not ready" paralysis
**Time required**: 30–45 minutes
**Archetype fit**: All archetypes; particularly powerful for Doers who dismiss their history

**Full script**:
> "Write down 10 things you have accomplished that, at the time you started them,
> you did not know how to do. They don't have to be impressive. They have to be real.
> For each one, note: what capability did completing it prove you have?
> Now look at the goal you're facing. Which of those proven capabilities applies —
> even partially?
> Notice: you have built things before without knowing how. What's actually different this time?"

**What to bring back**: The 3 past accomplishments most relevant to the current challenge.
**Debrief question**: "After this audit — do you still believe you're unequipped?"

---

### K2 — The Preparation Map
**Best for**: Genuine skill gap that needs structured closing — not avoidance dressed as preparation
**Time required**: 30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Thinker, Doer, Strategist

**Full script**:
> "Draw three columns: 'What I Need', 'What I Already Have', 'The Gap'.
> In Column 1: list every resource, skill, contact, or knowledge area you'd need
> to pursue this goal with confidence.
> In Column 2: honestly assess what you already have in each category.
> In Column 3: identify only the true gaps — not the nice-to-haves, the actual blockers.
> For each real gap: what is the smallest action that would begin to close it?
> One action per gap. Start with the smallest one."

**What to bring back**: The smallest first action toward the most critical gap.
**Debrief question**: "Which gap, if closed, would make all the others feel manageable?"

---

### K3 — The Role Model Frame
**Best for**: Capability doubt tied to self-image; "I'm not the kind of person who..."
**Time required**: 20 minutes setup; practice over one week
**Archetype fit**: Caregiver, Seeker, anyone with identity block underneath a capability block

**Full script**:
> "Think of someone — real or historical — who has done what you want to do.
> Someone you genuinely admire and whose approach resonates with you.
> For one week, when you face a challenge in this domain, ask:
> 'What would [name] do here?'
> Not what they would do in general — specifically in this moment, with this obstacle.
> This is not pretending to be them. It's using their example as a proof of possibility
> until your own confidence catches up with your capability.
> Notice: where does their approach differ from your instinct? What does that gap reveal?"

**What to bring back**: One specific moment where you asked the question — and what happened.
**Debrief question**: "What did they have that you were telling yourself you didn't?"

---

### K4 — The Minimum Viable Attempt
**Best for**: Waiting to be "ready" — needs to learn through doing, not through more preparation
**Time required**: Define a 48–72 hour experiment
**Archetype fit**: Doer, anyone in preparation paralysis

**Full script**:
> "Identify the smallest possible version of the thing you're not feeling capable of.
> Not the whole goal — just the first 10% of it.
> Complete only that in the next 72 hours.
> Don't aim for quality. Aim for completion.
> Write three sentences afterward: what happened, what you learned, what surprised you.
> Bring that back. Your capability assessment will be more accurate after doing
> than before doing."

**What to bring back**: The three sentences.
**Debrief question**: "Now that you've done the first 10% — what do you know that you didn't before?"

## COURAGE EXERCISES

### U1 — The Fear Inventory
**Best for**: Vague fear blocking action — needs to be named and examined specifically
**Time required**: 20–30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Thinker, Caregiver (frame as risk assessment for Thinkers)

**Full script**:
> "Write at the top of a page: 'I am afraid that...'
> Complete the sentence 10 times. Don't edit. Don't make it rational.
> Write whatever comes, even if it sounds dramatic or irrational.
> After you have 10 completions, read through them and ask of each:
> 1. Is this fear realistic or imagined?
> 2. If it happened — could I survive it?
> 3. How likely is it, honestly, on a scale of 1–10?
> Circle the one fear that, if dissolved, would free you most.
> That is the one we work with next."

**What to bring back**: The circled fear and your assessment of it.
**Debrief question**: "What would you do the day after the feared outcome happened?"

---

### U2 — The Pre-Mortem
**Best for**: Doers and Strategists who catastrophize — fear of failure blocking action
**Time required**: 30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Thinker, Doer, Strategist

**Full script**:
> "Imagine it is 12 months from now. You took the action you're afraid of taking.
> It went badly — not catastrophically, but it didn't work.
> Write a one-page account of what happened. Be specific. What exactly went wrong?
> Who knows? What does it cost you?
> Now read it back and answer: Could you survive this? What would you do next?
> Often what we discover: the worst realistic outcome is survivable.
> The fear is larger than the actual risk."

**What to bring back**: Your honest assessment of survivability.
**Debrief question**: "Now that you've lived the worst case on paper — what changes?"

---

### U3 — The Commitment Declaration
**Best for**: Chronic delay; readiness scores that never reach 8; someone who is ready but won't commit
**Time required**: 5 minutes to write, 48 hours to honor
**Archetype fit**: All archetypes — particularly Seeker (who over-explores), Doer (who over-pivots)

**Full script**:
> "Write one sentence that begins: 'By [specific date and time], I will have done [specific action].'
> Not 'I will try.' Not 'I will think about.' I will have done.
> Sign it. Put it somewhere you will see it daily.
> Tell one person whose opinion matters to you.
> Bring back the result — not the plan, the result."

**What to bring back**: Did you do it? If yes — what happened? If no — what stopped you?
**Debrief question**: "What did your follow-through (or lack of it) tell you about your actual commitment level?"

---

### U4 — The Conversation You're Avoiding
**Best for**: Interpersonal avoidance; courage block rooted in a relationship
**Time required**: Preparation 20 min; the conversation itself
**Archetype fit**: Caregiver (will over-prepare), Doer (will avoid or delay)

**Full script**:
> "Identify the one conversation you've been avoiding that would change something important.
> Before you have it, write:
> 1. What do I actually want from this conversation? (Not what I want to say — what outcome do I want?)
> 2. What am I afraid will happen if I have it?
> 3. What's the cost of not having it for another 3 months?
> Then have the conversation. Not perfectly — just have it.
> Write three sentences afterward: what happened, what surprised you,
> what you now know that you didn't before."

**What to bring back**: The three sentences.
**Debrief question**: "Was it as bad as you'd imagined? What does that tell you?"

---

### U5 — The 48-Hour Decision
**Best for**: Decision paralysis; someone who has enough information but keeps seeking more
**Time required**: 48 hours
**Archetype fit**: Thinker (needs a forcing function, tends to over-process)

**Full script**:
> "You have 48 hours to make this decision. Not forever — 48 hours.
> In those 48 hours, you are not allowed to:
> - Research further
> - Ask more people
> - Make a pros-and-cons list
> You are allowed to:
> - Notice what you feel
> - Pay attention to which option your body moves toward
> - Sleep on it once
> At the end of 48 hours, make the decision. Any decision.
> An imperfect decision made is better than a perfect decision never taken."

**What to bring back**: The decision — and how it felt to make it.
**Debrief question**: "Once you decided — did any part of you feel relief? Which direction?"

## IDENTITY EXERCISES

### I1 — The Inherited Belief Audit
**Best for**: Identity block rooted in family/cultural scripts; "people like me don't..."
**Time required**: 45 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Caregiver, Seeker, Integrator, anyone from strong cultural/family background

**Full script**:
> "Write the names of the 3–5 people who most shaped your understanding of what was
> possible for someone like you. Parents, teachers, community figures — whoever shaped
> the earliest version of your self-concept.
> For each person, write: what did they teach me, explicitly or by example,
> about what I could or couldn't achieve?
> Now look at the belief that's blocking you today.
> Whose voice is it? Did you choose this belief — or inherit it?
> Write: 'This belief belongs to [name]. I am choosing to [keep it / examine it / release it]
> because [your own reason, not theirs].'"

**What to bring back**: Whose voice it is and your decision about it.
**Debrief question**: "If that person had believed something different about you — how might you be different today?"

---

### I2 — The Future Self Letter
**Best for**: Identity gap between current self and the person required to achieve the goal
**Time required**: 30–45 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Seeker, Thinker, Caregiver, anyone doing identity transition work

**Full script**:
> "Imagine the version of you who has already achieved what you're working toward.
> Not a fantasy version — a grounded, real version.
> They are 3 years ahead of where you are today.
> Write a letter from them to you — now.
> What do they want you to know?
> What did they have to let go of to get there?
> What do they wish you'd do differently starting today?
> Write it in their voice — not your current voice. Their voice."

**What to bring back**: The one paragraph that felt most true.
**Debrief question**: "What did that future version of you have to believe that you don't yet believe?"

---

### I3 — The Role Inventory
**Best for**: Identity fragmentation; Integrator type overwhelmed by competing roles
**Time required**: 20 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Integrator, Caregiver, anyone in major life transition

**Full script**:
> "List every role you currently play: professional title, family roles, social roles, internal roles
> (e.g., 'the responsible one', 'the provider', 'the peacekeeper').
> For each role, rate: [1–5] How much does this role reflect who I actually am?
> [1–5] How much energy does this role require from me?
> Circle any role that is high energy, low authenticity.
> Ask: what would happen if I performed this role 20% less?
> Who would that affect, and is that okay?"

**What to bring back**: The highest-energy, lowest-authenticity role.
**Debrief question**: "Who told you that role was yours to carry?"

## GRIEF & TRANSITION EXERCISES

### G1 — The Completion Ritual
**Best for**: Inability to move forward because something hasn't been properly closed
**Time required**: 45–60 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Caregiver, Seeker, anyone in major transition or loss

**Full script**:
> "What has ended that you haven't yet said goodbye to?
> A role, a relationship, a version of yourself, a future you had planned.
> Write it a letter. Tell it:
> - What it gave you
> - What you're grateful for
> - What you're angry about or sad about
> - Why you have to let it go
> - What you're taking with you from it
> Then, if it feels right — do something physical to mark the ending.
> Burn the letter. Plant something. Give something away.
> Grief needs a container. Give this one a shape."

**What to bring back**: What the ritual revealed.
**Debrief question**: "What can you do now that you couldn't do while you were still holding on?"

---

### G2 — The Identity Bridge
**Best for**: Between-identity paralysis — left the old, not yet in the new
**Time required**: 30 minutes over 3 days
**Archetype fit**: Seeker, Integrator, Thinker, anyone in career/life pivot

**Full script**:
> "Day 1: Write who you were in the role or identity that has ended.
> What did you value? What were you known for? What did you build?
> Day 2: Write who you are becoming — even if you can only see it dimly.
> What matters to you now? What are you moving toward?
> Day 3: Write the bridge between them.
> What from who you were is worth carrying? What must be left behind?
> What is continuous — the thread that connects both versions?"

**What to bring back**: The thread. The continuous element.
**Debrief question**: "If that thread is what's truly you — how does it show up in where you're going?"

---

### G3 — The Kintsugi Mapping
**Best for**: Difficulty finding meaning in loss or failure — needs to see breakage as formative
**Time required**: 30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Doer (who sees failure as defeat or rebuilds without processing)

**Full script**:
> "Draw a simple outline of a vase or bowl on a blank page.
> In the cracks — draw the significant losses, failures, endings, or difficult passages
> of your life. Label each one.
> Now: for each crack, write in gold (or red, or whatever feels like gold to you)
> what that fracture gave you — what strength, perspective, or quality emerged from it.
> Stand back and look at the whole.
> This is you — not despite the breaks, but including them.
> What does someone who has been broken and repaired with gold know
> that someone who hasn't been broken yet doesn't?"

**What to bring back**: The crack that gave you the most valuable gold.
**Debrief question**: "Would you trade that fracture away — knowing what came from it?"

## LEADERSHIP EXERCISES

### L1 — The Team Mirror
**Best for**: Leader whose team is underperforming; can't see their own contribution to the pattern
**Time required**: 1 week of observation (2-day focused version available)
**Archetype fit**: Strategist, Doer (in leadership roles)

**Full script**:
> "For the next week, observe your team interactions without trying to fix anything.
> Notice: Where does energy increase? Where does it die?
> What behaviors — in you — precede the team's best moments?
> What behaviors — in you — precede the team's worst moments?
> Don't gather data about them. Gather data about you.
> Write 5 observations. Bring them back."

**2-day version**: Pick 2 days with significant team interaction. Same questions, compressed.
Tell the client: "2 days gives you a snapshot. A week gives you a pattern.
Start with 2 — and notice if the pattern holds."

**What to bring back**: The one observation that makes you uncomfortable.
**Debrief question**: "What would your team say if they were coaching you?"

---

### L2 — The Reverse Mentoring Session
**Best for**: Leader who has become disconnected from their team's reality
**Time required**: 2 structured conversations
**Archetype fit**: Strategist, Doer, any leader in seniority isolation

**Full script**:
> "Identify one person on your team — someone junior, someone whose reality is different from yours.
> Have two conversations with them:
> First: 'I'm trying to understand what it's like to work here from your perspective. 
> I'm not going to fix anything or respond defensively — I just want to understand.'
> Then actually listen. Take notes. Don't defend.
> Second conversation (1 week later): Tell them one thing you're changing based on what you heard.
> Bring back: what you heard that you hadn't known. And what it cost you to hear it."

**What to bring back**: The thing you hadn't known. And your honest reaction to hearing it.
**Debrief question**: "What has your position been protecting you from knowing?"

## BURNOUT & RESET EXERCISES

### B1 — The Subtraction Audit
**Best for**: Overwhelm, too many commitments, energy constantly depleted
**Time required**: 30 minutes
**Archetype fit**: Doer (says yes to everything), Caregiver (can't disappoint), Integrator (overextends)

**Full script**:
> "List everything you are currently committed to — professional, personal, relational.
> Everything.
> For each item, answer three questions:
> 1. Does this still serve what matters most to me?
> 2. Am I doing this from choice or from obligation/guilt?
> 3. If I could remove this without consequence — would I?
> Circle everything that got a 'no' to Q1 or Q2, or 'yes' to Q3.
> These are your candidates for subtraction.
> Choose one. Remove it this week."

**What to bring back**: What you removed and how it felt.
**Debrief question**: "What did you expect to feel — and what did you actually feel?"

---

### B2 — The Non-Negotiable Recovery Protocol
**Best for**: Someone running on empty who keeps postponing rest
**Time required**: Design in session; implement over 2 weeks
**Archetype fit**: All archetypes in burnout; especially Doers who treat rest as failure

**Full script**:
> "Design your minimum viable recovery — not your ideal recovery, your minimum.
> Answer:
> 1. What is the one physical practice (sleep, movement, food) that makes the biggest difference
>    to your energy when you maintain it?
> 2. What is the one mental/spiritual practice that most reliably restores your clarity?
> 3. What is one relational interaction per week that fills you rather than depletes you?
> Now: schedule all three. Not as aspirational goals — as non-negotiable appointments.
> Treat them with the same seriousness as a client meeting.
> Two weeks. Report back."

**What to bring back**: Whether you honored the appointments — and what happened to your energy.
**Debrief question**: "What had you been telling yourself was the reason you couldn't protect this time?"

---

### B3 — The One Thing
**Best for**: So overwhelmed that any action feels impossible; decision paralysis from exhaustion
**Time required**: 5 minutes
**Archetype fit**: All archetypes in acute overwhelm; particularly Integrators

**Full script**:
> "Do not make a list. Do not plan. Do not organize.
> Answer only this question:
> 'If I could only do ONE thing today — not the most important thing ever,
> just today — what would it be?'
> Write it on a Post-it.
> Do only that. Nothing else counts today.
> Tomorrow: repeat."

**What to bring back**: What the one thing was. Whether you did it. How you felt afterward.
**Debrief question**: "What happened to the other things on the imaginary list while you focused on one?"
